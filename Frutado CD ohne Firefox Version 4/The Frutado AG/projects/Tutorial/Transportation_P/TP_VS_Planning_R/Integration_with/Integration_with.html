Integration with Deployment

The different components of the supply chain have their own objectives which often can be conflicting, and changes in one part of the supply chain can affect the performance of another part. It is therefore a need for integrating and coordinating the different parts of the supply chain.

For the TP/VS module of the Frutado scenario it is relevant to look at the integration between deployment and TP/VS. Transportation planning is a detailed planning method, whereas deployment planning is made on a more aggregated level. This can lead to various capacity related problems, which can be adjusted by using an integration scheme.

As described before, vehicle resources are used in TP/VS planning to transport orders. Vehicle resources are used for detailed planning to map the capacity and availability of vehicles, where the available capacity is not dependent on time. In deployment, transportation resources are used for its aggregated planning where the capacity ensures feasibility of the deployment plan. Transportation resources are bucket resources, meaning that it has a quantity-oriented capacity which can be planned on daily basis. When integrating the two modules, then after running TP/VS planning feedback can be provided back to deployment to revise the weight capacity of the transportation resources.

In this lesson you will learn how to integrate the two planning modules. It will be shown how to calculate an adjustment factor, after running TP/VS, which is used to adjust the capacity of the transportation resources in deployment.
