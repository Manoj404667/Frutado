Product Allocation

In reality unfortunately unpredictable problems can lead to critical situations in order processing. 

Problems such as breakdowns in production, increased demand as well as shifted or reduced supply must be brought under control before they arise. 

Product allocations provide companies an opportunity to avoid these critical situations in order processing. 

In cases where demand or expected demand exceeds the supply an equal allocation of products and quick reactions to bottlenecks and changing market situations must be ensured.

By means of product allocations you can allocate products for specific customers or regions depending on the period. It is very important to know that allocations are not reservations for actual receipts, but limitations for the sales order confirmation.

Allocations help to avoid that the entire available quantity gets to the first customer and ensure that customers or customer groups are provided with their adequate contingent according to the sales and marketing policies. Generally allocations try to avoid "first come first serve" as this leads to delays of subsequent sales order confirmations or makes confirmation impossible.

Basically you can allocate material according to any criteria. The availability check is successful if there is a product allocation for a specific transaction in the relevant period and this product allocation has not yet been consumed by other requirements.
